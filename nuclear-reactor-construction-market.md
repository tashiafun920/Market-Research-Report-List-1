<p><p>The nuclear reactor construction market comprises several key types: Boiling Water Reactors (BWR), Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR), and others. BWRs generate steam directly in the reactor core, promoting simpler designs but requiring more extensive containment systems. PWRs maintain water under pressure to prevent boiling, allowing for higher efficiency and safety. Other reactor types include gas-cooled and liquid-sodium reactors, which cater to specific needs and technologies. Together, these variants address diverse energy demands and safety considerations in nuclear power generation.</p></p>
