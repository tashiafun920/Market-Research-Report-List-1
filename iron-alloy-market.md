<p><p>The iron alloy market is categorized into high carbon, medium carbon, and low carbon segments based on carbon content. High carbon alloys, containing over 0.6% carbon, are known for hardness and strength, primarily used in tools and wear-resistant applications. Medium carbon alloys, with 0.3% to 0.6% carbon, offer a balance of strength and ductility, making them suitable for structural components. Low carbon alloys, containing less than 0.3% carbon, are malleable and weldable, ideal for automotive and construction applications.</p></p>
