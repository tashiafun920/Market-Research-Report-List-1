<p><p>The PEG-based surgical sealant market features different types of polyethylene glycol (PEG) structures, each suited for specific applications. "4-Arm PEGs" consist of four reactive arms, enabling effective crosslinking for tissue adhesion. "8-Arm PEGs" offer enhanced flexibility and surface interaction due to their eight reactive sites, improving stability and performance. "Multi-Arm PEGs" encompass various configurations, often with more than eight arms, providing tailored rheological properties and adhesion capabilities, making them versatile for diverse surgical applications and promoting optimal healing outcomes.</p></p>
