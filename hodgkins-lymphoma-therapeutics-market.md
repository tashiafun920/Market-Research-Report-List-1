<p><p>The Hodgkin's lymphoma therapeutics market comprises various drug classes, each targeting cancer cells through distinct mechanisms. Alkylating antineoplastic agents damage DNA, inhibiting cell replication. Antibiotic antineoplastic agents, like bleomycin, disrupt RNA and DNA synthesis. Vinca alkaloids interfere with mitosis, while podophyllotoxin derivatives prevent cell division. Antimetabolites mimic natural substances, undermining DNA synthesis. Anthracycline agents, such as doxorubicin, induce apoptosis. Antimicrotubular agents disrupt chromosome separation, and corticosteroids reduce inflammation and improve patient tolerance to other treatments.</p></p>
