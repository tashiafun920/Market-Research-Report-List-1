<p><p>The seizure treatment market comprises two primary types: anticonvulsant therapy and surgical interventions. Anticonvulsant therapy involves the use of medications to control and reduce seizure frequency, often being the first line of treatment for epilepsy. Common medications include phenytoin, valproate, and lamotrigine. Surgical options are considered when medication fails to provide adequate control; procedures may involve resecting seizure foci or employing neuromodulation techniques. Both approaches aim to enhance patient quality of life through effective seizure management.</p></p>
