<p><p>RNA analysis in transcriptomics involves various market types, including microarrays, sequencing, polymerase chain reaction (PCR), and RNA interference (RNAi). Microarrays enable the simultaneous measurement of thousands of RNA expressions, while sequencing provides detailed information on RNA sequences for comprehensive analysis. PCR amplifies specific RNA segments for targeted study, facilitating detection and quantification. RNA interference is a method for gene silencing, helping to understand gene function by observing the effects of reduced gene expression on cellular processes.</p></p>
